Onboarding
Welcome to the South Brunswick CoderDojo team! Please follow the below steps to get caught up with the organization.
Step One: Join the Mentor Discord Server
Please check your mentor acceptance email from us. You should have received an invite to our mentor Discord server.
If your email does not contain the link, please email newjersey_sbpl.us@coderdojo.com.
If you do not have a Discord account, please make one on their website
Once you join the server, you will be in a “verification” period. We implemented this to ensure security. Once our moderators verify you, you will gain access.
Step Two: Review The General Articles
To become up to date with our Dojo, please read the articles in the “General” section of this knowledge base.
Step Two: Follow Any Event-Based Instructions Listed in the Server
Some events involve some special instructions. For example, you may need to assign yourself a group because of scheduling concerns.
Make sure to follow the steps in the #announcements text channel.
Step Three: Request Your Recommendation Lettter
We require all accepted applicants to send us a recommendation letter from a teacher.
This is to affirm to the library that you are knowledgable and capable of teaching computer science.
See this article for more information:
Recommendation Letters
Step Four: Start Tracking Your Hours
All mentors are responsible for tracking their own hours on a spreadsheet.
At the end of a session, we certify the hours that you tracked.
For more information, please read Certifying Hours.
Step Five: Review the Sushi Card Content
Please read the article called Using Sushi Cards.
We try to finish the first section of every sushi card before a session begins. Your duty as a mentor is to learn the content of the sushi cards well enough to teach it to others.
We recommend making the projects yourselves as practice, but relying on previous knowledge may suffice.
If you have any suggestions, please relay them to us on Discord! We consider and appreciate all feedback.
